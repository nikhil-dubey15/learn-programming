# 5.4 The Application Tab: The Browser's Safe

We've reached the final developer tool! Let's talk about how the browser remembers who you are.

When you log into Amazon, you don't have to type your password again every time you click a new product. If you close the tab and come back tomorrow, you are still logged in. How?

Because the browser has a tiny storage unit (a Safe) inside it. 

**The Application Tab** lets you peek inside that safe.

## The 3 Main Types of Storage

### 1. Cookies 🍪
* **What they are:** Tiny text files literally invented in the 1990s. 
* **How they work:** When you log in, the server gives you a Cookie (a long string of random letters/numbers). The browser stuffs it in your pocket. Every time you ask the waiter for a new page, your browser automatically shows the waiter the Cookie so they know it's you.
* **Size:** Very small. Mostly just used for tracking and login sessions.

### 2. Local Storage
* **What it is:** A much larger locker for the website to store data permanently on your hard drive. 
* **How it works:** Even if you completely shut down your computer and turn it back on a month later, whatever is in Local Storage will still be there! 
* **Common Use:** Saving your preference for "Dark Mode", or saving a half-written blog post so you don't lose your work if the internet drops.

### 3. Session Storage
* **What it is:** The exact same thing as Local Storage, but with one massive difference: **It has amnesia.**
* **How it works:** Data is only saved for the *current tab*. The moment you close the tab, everything in Session Storage is permanently deleted.

## 🛠️ Practical Exposure: Viewing your Cookies

Let's look inside your browser's safe.

1. Open **Google Chrome** and go to `www.youtube.com`.
2. Open DevTools (`Right-Click -> Inspect`) and find the **"Application"** tab. *(If your screen is small, you might need to click the `>>` arrows at the top to find it).*
3. On the left sidebar, find the **"Storage"** section. 
4. Click the little arrow next to **"Cookies"** to expand it, and click on `https://www.youtube.com`.
5. Look at the giant table of data! This is everything YouTube is using to remember who you are and what you've watched.
6. Now click on **"Local Storage"** on the left. See how much data they are saving permanently to your computer!
7. **A fun QA Trick:** In the Cookie table, you can right-click any row and click "Delete". If you delete your login cookie, the website will think you logged out instantly!
