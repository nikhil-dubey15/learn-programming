# 6.2 Meeting with Mentors: How to Ask for Help

You have direct access to developers with 10-20 years of experience. This is your greatest superpower! However, senior developers are often very busy. 

To get the most out of them, you need to learn **how to ask good technical questions.**

## The Golden Rule of Asking Questions
Never just say: *"It's broken, help me."* or *"I don't understand this."*

Instead, use the **3-Part Framework**:
1. **Goal:** Here is exactly what I am trying to do.
2. **Context/Action:** Here is exactly what I tried so far.
3. **The Block:** Here is the specific result/error I got that I don't understand.

**Example of a Bad Question:**
> *"Hey, Git isn't working on my laptop. Help?"*

**Example of a Great Question:**
> *"Hey, I'm trying to upload my new branch to GitHub (Goal). I ran `git commit` and then `git push origin new-branch` (Action). But my terminal threw an error saying 'Updates were rejected because the remote contains work that you do not have locally' (The Block). Can you help me understand what that means?"*

## Topics to Discuss Today

Bring this specific list to your mentors today. Keep the conversation focused on Day 1 topics.

### 1. Show them your Terminal skills
* Open your Terminal in front of them and show them you know how to `cd`, `mkdir`, and `touch`.
* **Question for them:** *"What are the 2 or 3 terminal commands you use the absolute most in your daily job?"*

### 2. Talk about Git Workflow at their company
* **Question for them:** *"Today I learned about Git branches and Pull Requests. At your company, what is the exact step-by-step process a piece of code goes through from the moment a developer writes it, to the moment it goes live to the customers?"*

### 3. Ask about DevTools in the real world
* Tell them you played with the Elements, Console, and Network tabs.
* **Question for them:** *"When someone reports a bug on the live website, which DevTools tab do you usually open first to investigate it? Do you look at the Console for red text, or the Network Tab for a failed 500 error?"*

### 4. Review Your Weak Points
* Look at the questions you struggled with in the **6.1 Review** file. 
* Read the question to them and ask: *"I struggled to understand this concept today. Can you explain it to me using a different analogy?"*
