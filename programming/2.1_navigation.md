# 2.1 Navigation: Moving Around the House

Welcome to the Terminal! The Terminal is just a different, text-based way to do everything you already do with your mouse. 

**The Analogy: Your Computer is a House**
Imagine your computer is a massive house with many rooms (folders). When you open the Terminal, you are standing in your "Home" room. 

Let's learn how to look around and walk into other rooms.

## The 3 Magic Commands

### 1. `pwd` (Print Working Directory)
* **What it means in English:** "Where am I standing right now?"
* **How to use it:** Just type `pwd` and press Enter.
* **What happens:** The Terminal will print the exact path to the "room" you are currently in. For example: `/Users/nikhildubey`. 

### 2. `ls` (List)
* **What it means in English:** "What is inside this room?"
* **How to use it:** Type `ls` and press Enter. 
* **What happens:** The Terminal will list all the files and folders in your current room. You might see things like `Desktop`, `Documents`, `Downloads`, etc.
* **Pro-Tip:** Try typing `ls -a`. This shows *hidden* things in the room (files that start with a dot, like `.Trash`).

### 3. `cd` (Change Directory)
* **What it means in English:** "Walk into that room."
* **How to use it:** Type `cd` followed by a space, and then the name of the folder you want to enter.
  * Example: `cd Desktop` (Takes you to the Desktop folder).
  * Example: `cd ..` (This is special! The two dots mean "walk backwards out of this room").
  * Example: `cd ~` (The tilde takes you straight back to your Home room, no matter where you are).

---

## 🛠️ Practical Exposure: Let's Go for a Walk!

1. Open your **Terminal** app.
2. Type `pwd` and hit Enter. *See where you are starting.*
3. Type `ls` and hit Enter. *Look at the folders available.*
4. Type `cd Desktop` and hit Enter. *You just walked onto your Desktop!*
5. Type `pwd` again. *Notice how your path changed.*
6. Type `ls`. *You should see all the files currently sitting on your actual Mac screen.*
7. Type `cd ..` to step backward into your Home folder.
